Welcome!

I am an Associate Professor at INRS-EMT, working in the INRS-UQO Joint Research Unit in Cybersecurity. My research interest is three fold. I work on strategies to minimize the transmission of sensitive data from edge devices to the cloud. The main goal is to decentralize deep learning AI from the cloud by enabling efficient training and inference of AI algorithms on low-power devices. My second research interest lies on the use of successful pre-trained language models, such as BERT, GPT and T5, combined with knowledge graph to detect malicious intentions and prevent semantic cyberattacks. My third research interest is focused on mitigating spoofing attacks, such as replays and speech synthesis, on authentication systems.

Previously, I was a researcher scientist in natural language and speech processing at Huawei Technologies Ltd., Noah’s Ark Lab, where I worked on projects related to model compression, low-latency and robustness of spoken language understanding.
